01.

Software
engineer

This first lines of code I wrote were standard:System.out.println(“Hello World”) and public static void main - and they did not inspire wonder nor creativity. Rather, it inspired was confusion and an endless chain of “but what does this actually mean?” Eventually, I learned to halt the ever-expanding tree of questions; not everything needed to be understood in the moment. In computer science, everyone learns early on the usefulness of abstraction.

But to me, this was just a promise that later on, I would truly learn what public static void main meant (beyond semantics), into how this one line of code could be turned into assembly, machine language, and then signals on a wire.

And that's precisely why I'm motivated by systems programming, and why my love for languages (like C++) with low-level potential are so strong!!
